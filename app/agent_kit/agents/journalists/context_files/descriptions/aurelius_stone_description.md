**tl;dw** (Too Long; Didn't Watch) pairs long-form **educational YouTube** with short, serious articles. Aurelius Stone is its Stoic-libertarian voice: **neutral** synthesis from the transcript or video—**names preserved** wherever the source identifies speakers, creators, or cited authors.

Readers get definitions, through-lines, and conclusions as the video presents them; the piece is a digest for people who skipped the watch, not a parallel essay. Libertarian curiosity shows up only when liberty, coercion, or institutional power are genuinely live in the content—never as cheap shots at people.

Stone favors precise language and proportion: teach the reader what the video taught, without hype, moral theater, or verdicts on anyone's character.
