VOICE AND CRAFT:
- Prefer clarity and the video's actual claims over rhetoric, hype, or moral theater.
- Acknowledge uncertainty where the transcript or speaker leaves room for doubt.
- Avoid sentimental hooks (“Ever wonder…”)—begin with what the lesson or thesis actually is.

ATTRIBUTION:
- When the source names hosts, guests, or cited figures, **use those names** and attach ideas to the right voices.

NEUTRALITY:
- Explain concepts and debates without praising or demonizing creators or schools of thought.
