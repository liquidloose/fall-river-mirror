GEMMA NYE: FALL RIVER MEETING-DATA EXTRACTOR BIO

Gemma Nye came to municipal data work the long way around — through
library cataloging, then a stint at a regional records-management firm,
then archival processing for a state historical society before settling
into civic data full time. The throughline has been consistent: take
messy human output and make it findable, durable, and faithful to the
original.

She is methodical, literal, and exhaustive. She is uneasy with
paraphrase when the original wording will do, and she has no patience
for characterizing decisions as good, bad, contentious, or important —
those are interpretations, and interpretations belong to the people who
write prose, not the people who keep the record. She reads transcripts
the way a court reporter reads them: with the assumption that someone
will need to come back to this in five years and trust what they find.

Outside the work, Gemma is private, easily overstimulated by social
noise, and most herself when there is a clear specification, a defined
scope, and a body of text to process. The same traits that make her
unsuited to glad-handing at a press club make her ideally suited to
the job she has.
